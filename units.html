Lesson IV (Computer for Communications)

Privacy Activity: for this mini project we had to get in groups and prepare a 3-5 minute presentation on our assigned scenario. In our presentation we had to answer the following:

1. Names of everyone if your group

2. The scenario you were assigned

3. In your scenario, did the people have the right to use the information they did or should it have been private? Why?

4. Are the people who were affected by the use of this information at fault? Why?

5. Give another example of something unexpected happening because of information shared on sites such as Facebook, Twitter, Snapchat, Instagram, blogs, etc.

6. Think about what data you have made available in different places/spaces such as Facebook, Twitter, texting, Netflix, email, Snapchat, Instagram, at the grocery store, etc. What might other people think about who you are based on this data? Is it an accurate impression of who you are?

7. Consider each of the following broad categories of societal change:a. Privacy
b. Safety
c. Globalization
d. Connectivity (keeping in touch with people)
e. Permanence of historical information

Lesson III (Applying the Problem Solving Process)

Handshake and Fencepost Activity:  for this activity we had to get into groups and solve the following problems. 
  1. Handshake Problem #1: Assume there are 20 people in a room, including you. You must shake hands with everyone else in the room. How many hands will you shake? If there are N (where N > 0) people in the room, how many hands will you shake?

  2. Fence Post Problem: You need to build one side of a fence that is 12 yards long. This fence will be built with fence posts and rails that connect one fence post to another. If each fence post is 1 yard away from the next fence post, how many fence posts will be needed for this side of the fence? How many fence posts will be needed for a side of a fence that is N (where N > 0) yards long?
